My Favorite Chocolate Cupcakes with Chocolate Cream Cheese Frosting

Life is sweeter with cupcakes!

This recipe evolved over the years from a cake recipe I found in an old cookbook.
The recipe was called "Wacky Cake" because it's a bit untraditional: no eggs are needed, and all the ingredients are stirred together at once.
Somehow, it makes the world's best chocolate cake, with a flavor and consistency reminiscent of a Hostess cupcake.

Chocolate Cream Cheese Frosting was a random find in another cookbook. It's like regular frosting turned up a notch. And it pairs perfectly with these cupcakes!

I can almost guarantee that you'll have all the supplies already on hand. Here are the ingredients you will need... let's get started!

For 12 cupcakes:

  • 1 1/4 cup flour
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1/3 cup cocoa powder
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 cup warm water
  • 1/3 vegetable oil
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 tsp apple cider vinegar

For the Chocolate Cream Cheese Frosting:

  • 1/2 cup butter
  • 4 oz. cream cheese
  • 1/3 cup cocoa powder
  • 3 cups powdered sugar
  • 2 tsp vanilla etract
  • salt

Directions:

Preheat oven to 350 degrees. Line a 12-cup muffin tin with paper liners.

For the cupcakes: stir together the first 5 ingredients. Once fully incorporated, stir in the last 4 ingredients.

Portion batter evenly into prepared muffin tin. Bake 18-20 minutes, just until tops spring back when pressed.
Don't overbake!

Cool in pan for 5 minutes, then remove to finish cooling on a wire rack.

For the frosting: Using an electric mixer, whip butter and cream cheese together until fully incorporated.
Mix in cocoa powder and powdered sugar. Beat until well mixed. Mix in vanilla and a dash of salt.
Continue beating until frosting is of spreadable consistency.

Frost cooled cupcakes. Add sprinkles if desired!
